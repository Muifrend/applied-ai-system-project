# Nutrition Guidelines for Pets

## Dogs — Feeding Basics

- **Puppies (under 1 year)**: Feed 3-4 times per day with puppy-specific food. Switch to adult food around 12 months (large breeds may need puppy food until 18-24 months).
- **Adult dogs (1-7 years)**: Feed twice daily. Portion size depends on weight, breed, and activity level — typically 1-2 cups per 20 lbs of body weight per day.
- **Senior dogs (7+ years)**: Feed twice daily with senior-formula food that supports joint health and is lower in calories.
- **Fresh water** must be available at all times. Change water at least once daily.

## Cats — Feeding Basics

- **Kittens (under 1 year)**: Feed 3-4 times per day with kitten-specific food high in protein and fat.
- **Adult cats (1-10 years)**: Feed twice daily. Average indoor cat needs about 200-300 calories per day.
- **Senior cats (10+ years)**: May need more frequent, smaller meals. Monitor weight closely — both obesity and weight loss are concerns.
- Cats are obligate carnivores — their diet must be primarily animal protein. Avoid grain-heavy fillers.

## Birds — Feeding Basics

- **Parrots and parakeets**: A base diet of high-quality pellets (60-70%), supplemented with fresh fruits and vegetables (20-30%), and seeds/nuts as treats (5-10%).
- **Avoid**: avocado, chocolate, caffeine, alcohol, onions, and garlic — these are toxic to birds.
- Fresh water daily. Some birds enjoy bathing in shallow water dishes.

## Toxic Foods to Avoid (All Species)

- **Dogs**: Chocolate, grapes/raisins, xylitol, onions, garlic, macadamia nuts, alcohol, caffeine.
- **Cats**: Onions, garlic, chocolate, caffeine, alcohol, raw eggs, grapes/raisins, lilies (extremely toxic).
- **Birds**: Avocado, chocolate, caffeine, alcohol, fruit seeds/pits, onions, garlic.

## Weight Management

- Obesity is the most common nutritional problem in pets. It shortens lifespan and increases risk of diabetes, joint disease, and heart problems.
- Use a body condition score chart to assess your pet — you should be able to feel ribs without pressing hard.
- Treats should make up no more than 10% of daily caloric intake.
- If your pet needs to lose weight, reduce portions gradually (no more than 1-2% body weight per week) and increase exercise.
