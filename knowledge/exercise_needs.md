# Exercise Needs for Pets

## Dogs — Exercise Requirements

- **High-energy breeds** (Border Collie, Labrador, Husky, Australian Shepherd): 60-120 minutes of vigorous exercise daily. Include running, fetch, hiking, or agility training.
- **Medium-energy breeds** (Beagle, Cocker Spaniel, Bulldog): 30-60 minutes of moderate exercise daily. Walks, play sessions, and swimming work well.
- **Low-energy breeds** (Basset Hound, Shih Tzu, Pug): 20-30 minutes of gentle exercise daily. Short walks and indoor play are usually sufficient.
- **Puppies**: Multiple short play sessions (5 minutes per month of age, up to twice daily). Avoid long runs or jumps until growth plates close (around 12-18 months).
- **Senior dogs**: Maintain regular but gentler exercise. Shorter walks, swimming (low impact), and mental stimulation games.

## Cats — Exercise Requirements

- Indoor cats need at least 15-30 minutes of interactive play daily to prevent obesity and behavioral issues.
- Use wand toys, laser pointers (always end with a physical toy to "catch"), puzzle feeders, and climbing structures.
- Rotate toys regularly to maintain interest.
- Some cats enjoy leash walks outdoors — introduce harness training gradually.
- Cat trees and window perches provide passive enrichment and encourage jumping and climbing.

## Birds — Exercise Requirements

- Birds need daily out-of-cage time for flight and exploration — at least 1-2 hours in a safe, bird-proofed room.
- Provide climbing toys, swings, ladders, and foraging toys inside the cage.
- Flight is the best exercise for birds. If wings are clipped, provide extra climbing opportunities.
- Social interaction counts as mental exercise — talk to and engage with your bird regularly.

## Signs of Insufficient Exercise

- **Dogs**: Destructive chewing, excessive barking, hyperactivity, weight gain, attention-seeking behavior.
- **Cats**: Overgrooming, aggression, obesity, nighttime zoomies, furniture scratching.
- **Birds**: Feather plucking, screaming, repetitive behaviors (pacing, head-bobbing), aggression.

## Exercise Safety Tips

- Avoid exercising dogs in extreme heat — early morning or evening is best. Watch for signs of heatstroke: excessive panting, drooling, stumbling.
- Always provide fresh water during and after exercise.
- Build endurance gradually — don't go from no exercise to intense workouts overnight.
- Check paw pads for injuries after walks on hot pavement or rough terrain.
