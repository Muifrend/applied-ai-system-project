# Grooming Care for Pets

## Dogs — Grooming Routine

- **Brushing**: Frequency depends on coat type.
  - Short coat (Beagle, Boxer): Once per week.
  - Medium coat (Golden Retriever, Shepherd): 2-3 times per week.
  - Long coat (Shih Tzu, Maltese): Daily brushing to prevent mats and tangles.
  - Double coat (Husky, Malamute): Daily during shedding season, 2-3 times per week otherwise.
- **Bathing**: Every 4-8 weeks depending on breed and activity level. Use dog-specific shampoo — human shampoo disrupts their skin pH.
- **Nail trimming**: Every 2-4 weeks. If you can hear nails clicking on the floor, they need trimming. Cut before the quick (the pink area inside the nail).
- **Ear cleaning**: Weekly for floppy-eared breeds (Basset Hound, Cocker Spaniel). Check for redness, odor, or discharge — signs of infection.
- **Dental care**: Brush teeth 2-3 times per week with dog-specific toothpaste. Dental chews and professional cleanings help prevent periodontal disease.

## Cats — Grooming Routine

- **Brushing**: Short-haired cats benefit from weekly brushing. Long-haired cats (Persian, Maine Coon) need daily brushing.
- **Bathing**: Rarely needed — cats are excellent self-groomers. Only bathe if they get into something messy or have a skin condition.
- **Nail trimming**: Every 2-3 weeks. Use cat-specific clippers. Provide scratching posts to help naturally file nails.
- **Dental care**: Brush teeth weekly if possible. Dental treats and annual professional cleanings are recommended.
- **Hairball prevention**: Regular brushing reduces hairballs. Hairball-formula food and fiber supplements can also help.

## Birds — Grooming Routine

- **Bathing**: Mist with a spray bottle 2-3 times per week or provide a shallow bath dish. Birds enjoy bathing and it keeps feathers healthy.
- **Nail trimming**: As needed — perches with rough textures help keep nails filed naturally. If overgrown, a vet or experienced handler should trim.
- **Beak care**: A healthy diet and cuttlebone or mineral block usually keep the beak in good shape. Overgrown beaks may indicate health issues — consult a vet.
- **Wing clipping**: A personal choice — consult with an avian vet. If clipped, only the primary flight feathers should be trimmed.

## When to See a Professional Groomer

- Severe matting that cannot be safely brushed out at home.
- Breeds that require specific haircuts (Poodles, Schnauzers, Bichons).
- If your pet becomes extremely stressed during home grooming.
- Anal gland expression for dogs — typically done by a groomer or vet.
