# Vaccination Schedules for Common Pets

## Dogs — Core Vaccines

- **Distemper (DHPP combo)**: First dose at 6-8 weeks, boosters every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks old, then every 1-3 years.
- **Rabies**: First dose at 12-16 weeks, booster at 1 year, then every 1-3 years depending on local law.
- **Parvovirus**: Included in DHPP combo. Critical for puppies — highly contagious and often fatal without vaccination.
- **Bordetella (kennel cough)**: Recommended annually or every 6 months for dogs that board, attend daycare, or visit dog parks.
- **Canine influenza**: Optional but recommended for social dogs. Two initial doses 2-4 weeks apart, then annually.
- **Leptospirosis**: Recommended in areas with wildlife exposure. Two initial doses 2-4 weeks apart, annual booster.

## Cats — Core Vaccines

- **FVRCP (Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, Panleukopenia)**: First dose at 6-8 weeks, boosters every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks, then every 1-3 years.
- **Rabies**: First dose at 12-16 weeks, booster at 1 year, then every 1-3 years.
- **FeLV (Feline Leukemia Virus)**: Recommended for kittens and outdoor cats. Two initial doses 3-4 weeks apart, annual booster.

## Birds — Vaccination

- **Polyomavirus**: Recommended for young birds, especially parrots and budgies. First dose at 4 weeks, booster at 6-8 weeks.
- Most pet birds do not require routine vaccinations, but annual veterinary wellness exams are strongly recommended.

## General Guidelines

- Always consult your veterinarian for a schedule tailored to your pet's species, breed, age, and lifestyle.
- Keep a vaccination record for each pet and set reminders for upcoming boosters.
- Puppies and kittens should avoid public areas until their vaccination series is complete.
- Watch for mild side effects after vaccination: lethargy, reduced appetite, mild swelling at injection site. Contact your vet if symptoms persist beyond 24-48 hours.
